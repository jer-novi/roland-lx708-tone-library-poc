# **About This App**

The acoustic piano has a long history going back more than 300 years. Its deep, complex sound field is produced by the combination of elements that make up the construction of the instrument, including the strings, soundboard, iron frame, wood cabinet, and more. By adjusting the various sound mechanisms, piano technicians are able to alter the voice of a piano to suit specific venues, performers, or music styles. Unfortunately, the voicing adjustment gradually deteriorates over time, and must be readjusted by a technician at regular intervals for top performance.

Powered by modern technology, the sounds in a Roland piano are meticulously crafted in the digital realm based on the acoustic piano sounding process. Because the sound source is digital, there are no strings or soundboard like in an acoustic piano. The great benefit of this is that the sound is always perfect, allowing you to enjoy a top-condition piano experience every time you sit down to play a Roland piano.

In addition, the digital sound engine lets you fine-tune the sound elements of the instrument just like a piano technician does with an acoustic piano. With the Piano Designer app, you have complete control over the fine details of the piano tone, right down to each of the 88 individual notes. You can even save different setups, so you can use the app to change the piano's voicing instantly.

If you don't want to fine-tune the sound on your own, the Piano Designer app includes many built-in setups created by top-class piano tuners. Try the various setups to see which ones work best with your playing style or different musical genres.

# **Sound / Sound Board and Individual Note Voicing**

Parameters vary by piano model.

## **1\. Sound Edit**

### **Cabinet**

* **Lid:** Adjusts the extent to which the lid of the acoustic piano is open. Higher values produce a brighter and more open sound. (This parameter is not available on all models.)  
* **Cabinet Resonance:** Adjusts the resonance of the piano cabinet. Higher values produce a larger resonance.  
* **Soundboard Type:** Selects the soundboard of the acoustic piano you want to simulate. The characteristics of the sound will vary depending on the soundboard.  
* **Soundboard Behavior:** Whereas "Soundboard Type" changes the basic sound, this adjusts the resonance of the soundboard. Higher settings produce a larger resonance.

### **Hammer**

* **Hammer Noise:** Adjusts the sound of the hammer striking the strings of an acoustic piano. Higher settings produce a louder sound of the hammer striking the string.  
* **Hammer Response:** Adjusts the time from when you play the key until the piano sound is heard. Higher settings produce a slower response.

### **Strings**

* **Full Scale String Res:** Adjusts the sympathetic vibrations of the strings that occurs in an acoustic piano when a key is played. Higher settings produce louder sympathetic vibrations. (Higher values make the resonance longer and thicker.)  
* **String Resonance:** Adjusts the sympathetic vibrations of the strings when a key is pressed. Higher settings produce a louder sympathetic vibration.  
* **Duplex Scale:** Adjusts the sympathetic vibration of the strings of a grand piano's Duplex Scale. The Duplex Scale is a system of sympathetically vibrating strings sometimes used in acoustic pianos. In this system, portions of strings that are not actually struck by the hammers are allowed to vibrate in sympathy with the strings that are struck. Higher settings produce a louder sympathetic vibration.  
* **Dynamic Harmonic:** Adjusts the volume of sympathetic vibration produced when the key is released. Higher settings produce a louder sympathetic sound.

### **Damper**

* **Damper Noise:** Adjusts the sound of the damper contacting the string when you release a key on an acoustic piano. Higher values produce a louder damper sound.  
* **Damper Resonance:** Adjusts the sympathetic vibration of the strings and the resonance of the soundboard that occurs when you press the damper pedal. Higher values produce louder sympathetic vibration.

### **Keyboard**

* **Key Touch:** Adjusts the force required to play the keys. Lower settings make the keys feel lighter. Higher settings make the keys feel heavier. (This setting does not affect the actual weight of the keys. It changes the way the sound responds to your playing dynamics.)  
* **Key Off Resonance:** Adjusts the sympathetic vibration that occurs when you release a key on an acoustic piano. Higher settings produce a louder sympathetic vibration.  
* **Key Off Noise:** Adjusts the action noise that occurs when you release a key. Higher settings produce a louder action noise.

### **Tuning**

* **Master Tuning:** Adjusts the overall pitch of the piano. This setting is useful when playing in an ensemble with other instruments. The displayed value is the frequency of the A4 key.  
* **Temperament:** You can play classical pieces in the same temperament (tuning) that was used in the era when the piece was composed. Modern pianos use equal temperament, in which the pitch intervals of all semitones are equal. In the era of classical music, various other temperaments were used. You can experience the chords and harmonies as the composer intended them.  
  * **Equal:** The standard tuning for modern pianos. The pitch intervals of all semitones are equal.  
  * **Just Major:** This tuning produces perfect major chords. The sound is beautiful when playing in the major key that you selected using Temperament Key.  
  * **Just Minor:** This tuning produces perfect minor chords. The sound is beautiful when playing in the minor key that you selected using Temperament Key.  
  * **Pythagorean:** This tuning, devised by the philosopher Pythagoras, resolves the dissonance of fourths and fifths. It is suitable for melodies with simple chords, and for playing polyphony.  
  * **Kirnberger I:** As in the Just temperaments, the perfect chords are preserved in this tuning. Because fewer keys can be played in this tuning compared to Kirnberger II, it is suited to specific songs.  
  * **Kirnberger II:** A revision of the Kirnberger I tuning. This tuning allows more keys to be played.  
  * **Kirnberger III:** A revision of the Kirnberger II tuning. This tuning allows even more keys to be played, and is the most commonly used Kirnberger tuning.  
  * **Meantone:** This tuning was popular in the era of the Renaissance and the Baroque periods. It allows keys to be played in any key.  
  * **Werckmeister:** This tuning combines the Meantone and Pythagorean tunings. This tuning allows pieces to be played in multiple keys.  
  * **Arabic:** This tuning is suitable for the music of Arabia.  
* **Temperament Key:** When playing with a tuning other than equal temperament, you must specify the fundamental key of the song. If you select C as the key, the tuning will be matched to the C major (or C minor) scale. If you select A as the key, the tuning will be matched to the A major (or A minor) scale.

### **Other**

* **Ambience:** Adjusts the reverberation (ambience) applied to the piano sound. Higher settings produce more reverberation. The type of reverberation will vary depending on the selected piano sound. If the piano sound is changed, this setting will be updated to the appropriate value.  
* **Ear Sense:** Adjusts the change applied to the sound when using headphones. Higher settings produce a more spacious and natural sound.  
* **Headphone 3D Ambience:** Adjusts the spaciousness of the sound when you use headphones. Higher values produce a more spacious sound.  
* **Volume Limit:** This setting specifies the maximum volume. The volume will not exceed the level you specify here even if you raise the volume to the maximum.

## **2\. Individual Note Voicing**

* **Single Note Tuning:** Adjusts the pitch of each individual key.  
* **Single Note Character:** Adjusts the tonal character of each individual key. Lower values produce a softer sound; higher values produce a harder sound.  
* **Single Note Volume:** Adjusts the volume of each individual key. Lower values produce a quieter sound.

# **Let's edit piano tones\!**

## **Sound Edit**

Based on actual acoustic piano sound elements, the parameters in Sound Edit allow you to modify the instrument's overall tonal character. Many parameters provide subtle sound changes that will only be apparent with careful listening, while others like Lid and Cabinet Resonance offer drastic sound changes that are immediately noticeable.

## **Individual Note Voicing**

These powerful parameters—Single Note Tuning, Single Note Volume, and Single Note Character—let you adjust the sound of each note on an individual basis.

Here are some adjustment examples to get you started.

**Ex 1:** Make the sound of lower register notes more clear.

Using Note Volume, reduce the low, thicker register notes, and increase the Single Note Character a bit to achieve a tighter attack.

**Ex 2:** Soften the tone of lower register notes.

Reduce the Single Note Character a bit on lower register notes to get a milder tone.

**Ex 3:** Soften the tone of upper register notes.

Reduce Single Note Character settings for the desired note range. It also helps to use the Lid parameter to adjust the piano's virtual lid; this affects the brightness in the same way that adjusting the lid opening does on an acoustic piano. Adjust the Cabinet Resonance parameter to add more resonance to the sound.

## **Achieving the Best Results with Individual Note Voicing**

Individual Note Voicing allows you to control pitch, level, and character for each individual note. In use, these parameters are very interactive. For example, when you change the tonal character with the Single Note Character parameter, you may feel that the pitch has changed too, even if you haven't adjusted the tuning. This occurs due to the natural overtone changes introduced with the Single Note Character setting. You may also feel that the volume has changed as well.

Just as a skilled acoustic piano technician makes many different small adjustments throughout the instrument to fine-tune and balance the overall sound, you'll find that you get the most favorable results when adjusting the Individual Note Voicing parameters in combination.
